# Source Catalog

Select source types based on the topic's domain. For any single research session, pick 2–3 types most relevant to the topic and research type. Do not use all sources — choose what fits.

---

## Technical (libraries, tools, frameworks, architectures, algorithms)

- **Official documentation** — primary source; check version compatibility
- **GitHub** — repository issues, discussions, CHANGELOG, README; reveals real-world adoption and known problems
- **Hacker News / Stack Overflow** — community signals, practical gotchas, migration experience
- **Official engineering blogs** — deeper rationale behind design decisions
- **arXiv** — for algorithms, ML models, distributed systems, performance research
- **Benchmarks and performance studies** — prefer independent, reproducible benchmarks over vendor claims

For Validatory research on technical approaches: GitHub issues and failure post-mortems are especially high-signal.

---

## Market / Business (products, competitors, industry trends, adoption)

- **Company websites and press releases** — what they claim about themselves
- **Business and tech news** — TechCrunch, The Verge, Bloomberg Tech, Reuters for recent events and funding
- **Analyst reports** — Gartner Magic Quadrant, Forrester Wave, IDC (look for quoted data, not just conclusions)
- **Crunchbase / LinkedIn** — company size, funding, product changes
- **Hacker News, Reddit** — community sentiment, user complaints, adoption signals
- **Job listings** — reliable proxy for technology adoption and team composition

---

## Academic / Scientific

- **arXiv** — preprints across CS, physics, biology, economics; recent and fast
- **PubMed** — life sciences, medicine, health
- **Google Scholar** — cross-domain; good for finding highly-cited work
- **ACM Digital Library** — computing systems, HCI, programming languages
- **IEEE Xplore** — engineering, electronics, signal processing
- **Review articles and meta-analyses** — prefer these over individual studies for established topics; higher signal
- **Institutional reports** — universities, national labs, WHO, government agencies

---

## Life / Personal Decisions (health, finance, career, lifestyle)

- **Academic research** — PubMed, Google Scholar; look for systematic reviews
- **Government and institutional sources** — CDC, NHS, consumer protection agencies; authoritative for factual claims
- **Consumer Reports / independent reviews** — product safety, long-term reliability
- **Reddit communities and forums** — lived experience, edge cases, minority outcomes official sources won't surface
- **Expert practitioner blogs** — doctors, financial advisors, lawyers writing for general audiences

---

## General (any topic)

- **Wikipedia** — background and definitions only; not a citable source, but useful for orienting in an unfamiliar space and finding primary sources
- **Reputable news sources** — for recent events and current state
- **Primary sources always preferred** — original reports, official statements, raw data over summaries and aggregators

---

## Source selection by research type

| Research type | Emphasis |
|--------------|---------|
| Exploratory | Broader mix; start with overview sources to map the space before going deep |
| Directional | Practitioner experience and failure modes; what has and hasn't worked for others with the same goal |
| Comparative | Official docs + independent benchmarks + community real-world experience; triangulate across all three |
| Validatory | Primary authoritative sources + counter-evidence specifically; actively search for "why this approach fails" |
