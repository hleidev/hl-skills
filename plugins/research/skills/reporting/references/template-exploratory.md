# Report Template: Exploratory Research

Use this structure when the user wants to understand a space they are unfamiliar with.
The goal is a landscape map, not a decision. There are no options to compare yet.

---

## Report structure

### Background
3 sentences: who wants to understand this topic, in what context, and what gap in knowledge this research fills. Must be understandable by someone not in this conversation.

### What You Asked
Restate the user's original question or goal in one sentence. If the user's framing was imprecise or based on a wrong assumption, state the corrected framing here.

### Landscape Overview
A plain-language summary of the space: what it is, why it matters, current state of maturity or adoption. 3–5 sentences. No jargon without definition.

### Key Areas / Branches

#### \<Area 1 name\>
What this area is, who it's relevant for, current maturity. One short paragraph.

#### \<Area 2 name\>
...

Aim for 3–5 key areas. More than 5 is usually a sign the scope is too wide — narrow it or group related areas.

### Common Entry Points
Where someone new to this space typically starts: a specific tool, resource, concept, or community. Concrete and actionable. Not "explore the space further."

- ...

### Recommendation
**Start with**: \<specific concrete starting point\>
**Why**: \<one sentence tied to user's context\>
**After that**: \<what comes next, briefly\>

### Open Questions
Up to 2 questions that can only be answered with more context from the user. Omit section entirely if none.
Each question must be answerable in one sentence.
